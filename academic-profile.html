About Me

Thinking at the intersection of care, gender, and early education

I am a researcher and Master's graduate in Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) from Ambedkar University Delhi, where my work sits at the crossroads of feminist sociology, care theory, and educational policy in contemporary India.

My academic interests centre on how gender structures the labour of teaching and care in early childhood settings — examining what it means for women to do this work, how policy frameworks such as NEP 2020's Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN) shape those conditions, and what teacher subjectivity looks like within feminised, undervalued care professions.

I bring together theoretical rigour with attentiveness to everyday practice, drawing on feminist care ethics, socio-cultural theory, and qualitative methodologies to situate pre-primary teachers' experiences within broader structures of power and institutional change.

Alongside my research, I have worked across education policy, gender studies, and child rights — with organisations including NITI Aayog, CSPS, and CRY — and I continue to teach and facilitate in school and community settings.

Master's Research

Dissertation

"Pre-Primary Teachers in Private Schools: A Qualitative Study on their Understanding of FLN & Child Development"

This study examines how pre-primary teachers in private school settings in India understand and enact Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN) and child development, as framed by NEP 2020. Situated within feminist care ethics and policy enactment theory, it explores how teacher motivations, aspirations, and professional subjectivity shape what policy looks like in classroom realities.

Through in-depth qualitative interviews and feminist thematic analysis, the research surfaces how teachers navigate institutional pressures, parental expectations, and their own identities as care workers — offering insight into how gender mediates the translation of education reform into everyday practice.
